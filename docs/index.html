The chance that two people in the same room have the same birthday — that is the Birthday Paradox . And according to fancy math, there is a 50.7% chance when there are just 23 people+This is in a hypothetical world. In reality, people arent born evenly throughout the year, and leap years are excluded. However, the numbers should still be pretty close. More on this in the appendix. in a room. Because each time we get 23 people together, its basically a coin flip that we get a shared birthday. The more we repeat this, the more balanced our results will be. That is the Law of Large Numbers in action.

The odds can seem surprising because as self-involved humans , we usually frame the situation by comparing just ourselves to 365 possible days. Our brains are more inclined to think about things linearly, but as we add more people to a room, the number of comparisons actually goes up exponentially .
